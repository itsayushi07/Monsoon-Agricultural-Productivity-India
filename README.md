## Abstract

The Indian summer monsoon is a critical determinant of agricultural productivity and food security. Variations in rainfall intensity and distribution can significantly influence crop yields, particularly for rain-fed agricultural systems.

This project investigates the relationship between monsoon rainfall variability and agricultural productivity in India from 1980–2020. Using historical climate and agricultural datasets, statistical analyses were performed to quantify rainfall anomalies, evaluate crop yield trends, and assess correlations between precipitation patterns and agricultural output.

---

## Research Objectives

- Analyze long-term monsoon rainfall variability (1980–2020)
- Quantify rainfall anomalies and variability indicators
- Assess rice and wheat yield trends
- Evaluate climate–agriculture relationships using statistical methods
- Investigate the impact of rainfall fluctuations on crop productivity

## Key Findings

### Rainfall Variability
- Significant interannual variability observed across the study period.
- Multiple years exhibited substantial positive and negative rainfall anomalies.
- Long-term fluctuations highlight increasing climatic uncertainty.

### Agricultural Productivity
- Rice productivity demonstrated greater sensitivity to rainfall variability.
- Wheat productivity showed comparatively lower dependence due to irrigation support.
- Crop yields generally improved over time despite climatic fluctuations.

### Correlation Analysis
- Positive correlation observed between monsoon rainfall and agricultural productivity.
- Rainfall variability remains a significant factor influencing crop performance.
- Climate resilience strategies are increasingly important for sustainable agriculture.
