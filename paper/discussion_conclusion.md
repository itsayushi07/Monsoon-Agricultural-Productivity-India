# Discussion and Conclusion

The findings of this study confirm that monsoon rainfall variability significantly influences agricultural productivity in India. Despite technological advancements and irrigation expansion, agriculture remains sensitive to climatic fluctuations.

Rice shows higher sensitivity to rainfall variability compared to wheat due to its greater dependence on monsoon water supply. Wheat productivity is relatively more stable due to irrigation support.

The study also highlights that not only total rainfall but also its timing and distribution are critical for agricultural performance.

In conclusion, monsoon variability continues to be a major factor affecting Indian agriculture. Strengthening irrigation systems, adopting climate-resilient crops, and improving forecasting systems can help reduce vulnerability.
