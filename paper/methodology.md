# Methodology

## Research Design
This study uses a quantitative longitudinal research design to examine the relationship between monsoon rainfall variability and agricultural productivity in India from 1980 to 2020.

## Data Sources
- Rainfall data: India Meteorological Department (IMD)
- Agricultural data: FAOSTAT

## Variables
- Independent Variable: Monsoon rainfall (mm)
- Dependent Variables: Rice yield (kg/ha), Wheat yield (kg/ha)

## Statistical Methods
The following methods were used:
- Rainfall anomaly analysis
- Standard deviation (SD)
- Coefficient of variation (CV)
- Pearson correlation coefficient

## Rainfall Anomaly Formula
Anomaly (%) = ((Ai − M) / M) × 100

Where:
Ai = annual rainfall
M = long-term mean rainfall

## Purpose
These methods were used to understand rainfall variability and its relationship with agricultural productivity over time.
