# Abstract

This study investigates the impact of monsoon rainfall variability on agricultural productivity in India over the period 1980–2020. Using secondary data from the India Meteorological Department (IMD) and FAOSTAT, the study examines long-term trends in rainfall and crop yields for rice and wheat.

The analysis employs rainfall anomaly calculations, standard deviation, coefficient of variation, and Pearson correlation to evaluate climatic variability and its agricultural implications.

The results indicate significant inter-annual variability in monsoon rainfall, with noticeable drought and flood years affecting agricultural output. Rice yield shows a stronger sensitivity to rainfall variability compared to wheat, which benefits from irrigation support.

Overall, the study highlights that while agricultural productivity has increased over time, monsoon variability remains a key limiting factor in sustaining stable crop production in India.
